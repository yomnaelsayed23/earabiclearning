Islamic Studies Course

Online Islamic Studies Course: "Alim/Alimah Diploma"

 

Alim course is an Islamic educational program that is designed to teach students about the Islamic religion and its teachings. The word "Alim" is an Arabic term that means "scholar," and an Alim course is typically taken by students who want to become Islamic scholars or leaders.

 

The Alim course typically covers a wide range of topics related to Islam, including :

 

  • Al-Quran (Memorization , Tajweed , Tillawah)

  • Hadith (sayings and actions of Prophet Muhammad)

  • Islamic history

  • Islamic law

  • Islamic spirituality.

The length and content of an Alim course may vary depending on the institution offering it and the specific program. Some courses may take several years to complete, while others may be shorter in duration. Generally, Alim courses are designed to provide a comprehensive understanding of Islam and prepare students for leadership roles in Islamic communities.

The material, subjects, and study resources for an Alim course may vary depending on the institution offering it and the specific program. However, here are some common elements that are typically included:

 

1- Quranic Studies:  Alim course typically includes an in-depth study of the Quran, including its interpretation, commentary, and memorization. This may include the study of Arabic grammar and syntax, as well as the study of the science of Quranic exegesis (Tafsir).

 

2- Hadith Studies:  The course usually includes a comprehensive study of the Hadith literature, which consists of the sayings and actions of the Prophet Muhammad. This may include the study of the science of Hadith, including the classification and authentication of Hadith.

 

3- Islamic Jurisprudence:  The Alim course usually includes the study of Islamic law (Shariah) and its principles. This may include the study of Islamic legal theory (Usul al-Fiqh), the various schools of Islamic jurisprudence (Madhabs), and the application of Islamic law to various situations.

 

4- Islamic History:  The course typically covers the history of Islam and the Islamic civilization, including the life and times of the Prophet Muhammad and the early Muslim community, as well as the history of Islamic empires and the contributions of Muslim scholars and scientists.

 

 

5- Islamic Spirituality: 

Alim course usually includes the study of Islamic spirituality and the practice of Islamic rituals, including prayer, fasting, charity, and pilgrimage.

 

In terms of study resources, students in an Alim course typically use a variety of textbooks, lectures, and other materials to supplement their learning. Some common study resources include:

 

  • Islamic Textbooks: Students may use textbooks written by Muslim scholars to study various subjects, such as Quranic studies, Hadith studies, Islamic law, and Islamic history.

 

  • Lectures: Students may attend lectures given by Islamic scholars on various topics, or watch recordings of lectures online.

 

  • Online Resources: Students may use online resources, such as Islamic websites and online libraries, to access Islamic texts and other study materials.

 

  • Study Groups: Students may form study groups with their peers to review and discuss course material.

The course is usually taught in Arabic, which is the language of the Quran and the primary language of Islamic scholarship, However we still offer the course in English for non Arabic speaker we advise student to learn Arabic Fusha (Quranic Arabic) side by side with Alim course.

Overall, the Alim course is a rigorous and comprehensive program designed to provide students with a deep understanding of Islam and prepare them for leadership roles in Islamic communities.

We offer special courses for

  • Students who wish to learn Islamic studies
  • Students who wish to specialize in the faculties of the Islamic Dawah, law, Sharia, Islamic studies, Arabic studies or divinities.
