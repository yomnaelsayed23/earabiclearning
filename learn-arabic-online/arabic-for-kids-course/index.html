Kids Arabic Lessons (5 to 12 years old)

 


The younger your kid starts to learn Arabic , the easier it is to master it. These kids' Arabic courses help kids (6 to 12 years old) learn Arabic via reading kids Arabic stories and discussing topics. We develop a customized Arabic study plan for kids and the Arabic lessons are based on each child's learning style and pace. We teach kids practical Arabic skills that are most useful in everyday life.

 

Moreover, we offer a variety of interesting topics that make your kid learn Arabic with fun and high efficiency! Learning Arabic at eArabicLearning.com can be one of the best investments you make for your children's future.


In these Arabic classes, kids will receive systematic training in four aspects: listening, speaking, reading and writing. Our Kids Arabic Lessons are divided into 4 different levels, ranging from Beginner Level to Advanced level, which can help your kids achieve their Arabic learning goals step by step.

Kids Arabic Lessons Level 1

  1. This level is for kids who have learned just the Arabic Letters and just know how read Arabic letters And some basic words.
  2. In this level, your kids will learn to read and write Arabic letters and the basic phonetics of the Arabic language. They will learn basic daily vocabularies and short sentences, such as greetings, self-introduction, introduce family members, ages, colors, numbers, animals, fruits, etc. We will use fun pictures and games to make class fun and effective.
 

 


Kids Arabic Lessons Level 2

  1. This level is for kids who can use familiar words to answer simple questions in areas of basic daily topics.
  2. In this level, your kids will learn simple words, short sentences, and simple dialogues such as clothing, toys, household appliances, weather, etc. we will teach your kids some simple nursery rhyme, and your kids will learn to write very simple Arabic sentences.
 

 


Kids Arabic Lessons Level 3

  1. This level is for kids who can ask and answer questions with some longer sentences and who can narrate short stories. Your kids can exchange information clearly, although somewhat slowly.
  2. In this level, your kids will learn topics such as directions, hobbies, time, seasons, express feelings, likes/dislikes, countries, etc. Your kids will learn to narrate a short and simple story. More Arabic vocabularies and nursery rhyme will be learned.
 

 


Kids Arabic Lessons Level 4

  1. This level is for kids who can communicate with some confidence on familiar routine and non-routine matters related to their interests, interact with a degree of fluency and spontaneity. Your kids can read longer stories, folk tales, etc.
  2. In this level, your kids will learn topics such as express feelings, school life, hobbies, etc.
     
 eArabicLearning Academy uses a combination of methodologies to teach Arabic as a foreign language to kids. The lessons are designed to be interactive and engaging, using a variety of techniques such as games, songs, stories, and role-playing to make the learning experience fun and enjoyable for children. The lessons are designed to be age-appropriate and tailored to the specific needs and learning styles of each individual child.
 
The curriculum is based on the communicative approach, which emphasizes the use of real-life situations and tasks to develop the student's language skills. This approach encourages children to use the language they are learning in a natural and authentic way, which helps to build confidence and fluency.
 
The lessons are also designed to develop the four skills of listening, speaking, reading and writing in a balanced way. The teachers use interactive activities and multimedia resources to keep the children engaged and motivated. The teachers also use the target language in class as much as possible to create a language-rich environment and expose children to authentic language input.
 
In addition, the teachers use a variety of teaching techniques such as TPR (Total Physical Response) and TBL (Task-based learning) that help to create a dynamic and interactive learning environment.
 
          That sounds like a great approach to teaching Arabic to children. By incorporating Islamic and Arabic culture into the lessons, children will not only learn the language, but also gain a deeper understanding of the rich history and traditions of the Arabic-speaking world.

By emphasizing conversational skills, children will be able to apply their knowledge in real-life situations and communicate effectively with native speakers. This can be especially important for children who may have Arabic-speaking family members or who may travel to Arabic-speaking countries in the future.

Incorporating cultural education can also help children develop a sense of appreciation and respect for different cultures and traditions. This can promote cross-cultural understanding and empathy, which are important values in today's globalized world.
 
Overall, eArabicLearning Academy's approach to teaching Arabic as a foreign language to kids is designed to be fun, interactive, and engaging, while also providing a comprehensive education that will help children to develop their language skills and cultural understanding. With experienced teachers, and a curriculum that is tailored to the needs of each individual child, eArabicLearning Academy is an excellent resource for parents who want to give their children the opportunity to learn Arabic in a fun and effective way
